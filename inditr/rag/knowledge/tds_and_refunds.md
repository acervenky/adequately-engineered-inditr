# TDS, Advance Tax, and Refunds — AY 2026-27

## TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)

### Salary TDS — Section 192
- Employer deducts TDS monthly based on projected annual salary
- Employee must declare investments and deductions to employer for correct TDS computation
- Mismatch between Form 16 and 26AS is a common filing error — always reconcile
- If you switch jobs mid-year: both employers must be considered for total TDS; inform new employer of previous salary to avoid under-deduction and interest

### TDS on Interest — Section 194A (Budget 2025 — effective FY 2025-26)
- Banks/post offices deduct TDS at 10% on FD/RD interest if total interest from a bank > ₹50,000/year
- Budget 2025 raised the non-senior threshold from ₹40,000 to ₹50,000, now equal to the senior citizen limit
- Senior citizens: same ₹50,000 threshold (unchanged)
- If PAN not furnished: TDS at 20%
- Submit Form 15G (non-senior, total income below taxable limit) or 15H (senior citizen) to avoid TDS on interest
- Form 15G/15H must be submitted at the START of the financial year, not after TDS is deducted

### TDS on Dividends — Section 194 (Budget 2025 — effective FY 2025-26)
- 10% TDS if dividend income > ₹10,000 from a single company in the financial year
- Budget 2025 raised the threshold from ₹5,000 to ₹10,000
- Mutual fund dividend (IDCW): 10% TDS on each payment; applies if aggregate IDCW from a fund > ₹10,000/year
- Dividend income is fully taxable at slab rate (since FY 2020-21); TDS is a credit against your total liability

### TDS on Professional Fees — Section 194J
- 10% TDS on professional fees, technical services, royalty > ₹30,000

### TDS on Professional Fees / Freelance — Section 194J
- 10% TDS on professional fees, technical services, royalty > ₹30,000/year per payer
- Freelancers receiving payments from companies will have 10% TDS deducted; claim full credit in ITR
- If only technical services (not professional): 2% TDS (Budget 2020 change)

### TDS on Rent Paid by Individuals — Section 194-IB (Budget 2025 change)
- If an individual pays rent > ₹50,000/month to a resident landlord, TDS must be deducted
- TDS rate: **2%** (Budget 2025 reduced from 5%, effective FY 2025-26)
- Deduct TDS once a year (March) or at time of vacancy
- File Form 26QC online and issue Form 16C to landlord
- This applies to TENANT — the deduction is credited to landlord's PAN
- Landlord must include rent income; tenant claims no deduction for TDS deducted (it's the landlord's credit)

### TDS on Property Purchase — Section 194IA
- Buyer deducts 1% TDS on purchase price if > ₹50,00,000
- Buyer must file Form 26QB and issue TDS certificate (Form 16B) to seller
- Seller credits this TDS against their capital gains tax liability

## Form 26AS and AIS — Reconciliation

Always match TDS credits in Form 26AS with your income:
- Every rupee of TDS in 26AS must correspond to income in your return
- Missing TDS credit: raise grievance on IT portal or contact the deductor
- Wrong PAN mapping: request correction from deductor

## Advance Tax — Section 207 to 219

### Who Must Pay Advance Tax
- Individuals with estimated tax liability > ₹10,000 after TDS credit
- Including salary earners with additional income (capital gains, rent, freelance, interest)

### Advance Tax Due Dates
| Instalment | Due Date       | Cumulative % |
|------------|----------------|--------------|
| 1st        | 15 June 2025   | 15%          |
| 2nd        | 15 September 2025 | 45%       |
| 3rd        | 15 December 2025 | 75%        |
| 4th        | 15 March 2026  | 100%         |

Capital gains realised after the normal due date: advance tax can be paid by 31 March without 234C interest (except Q4 shortfall of more than 10%).

## Interest on Shortfall

### Section 234A — Late Filing
- 1% per month on unpaid tax for each month of delay beyond the due date (31 July 2026)

### Section 234B — Short Advance Tax
- 1% per month if advance tax paid < 90% of assessed tax
- Charged from April 1 of assessment year until date of filing

### Section 234C — Deferment of Advance Tax
- 1% per month for 3 months if instalment short by > 10%
- Not levied if capital gains arose after last instalment due date and full tax paid by 31 March

## Self-Assessment Tax

If any tax is remaining after TDS and advance tax, pay it as Self-Assessment Tax (SAT) before filing:
- Pay via IT portal: e-Pay Tax → Challan 280 → Self-Assessment Tax (code 300)
- Note the BSR code and challan serial number — enter in ITR

## Refund Process

### How Refunds Work
- Refund = TDS + Advance Tax + SAT − Total Tax Liability
- Processed after filing; typically within 7-30 days for e-verified returns
- Directly credited to bank account pre-validated on IT portal
- Interest on refund: 0.5% per month under Section 244A (if refund > 10% of tax payable)

### Speed Up Refund
- Pre-validate bank account on IT portal before filing
- File early in April/May (before peak July rush)
- Use e-verification (Aadhaar OTP is fastest)
- Ensure mobile number linked with Aadhaar is active

## PAN and Aadhaar

- PAN is mandatory for all taxpayers
- Aadhaar–PAN linking mandatory; returns cannot be processed without it
- Format: 5 alpha + 4 numeric + 1 alpha (e.g. ABCDE1234F)
- PAN must be masked in all user-facing documents: XXXXX####X (only last 4 digits + last alpha visible in outputs)
