# Tax Slabs, Rebates, and Surcharge — AY 2026-27 (FY 2025-26)

## New Tax Regime Slabs (Default from FY 2023-24)

The new regime is the default regime for AY 2026-27. No deductions (except standard deduction and 80CCD(2) employer NPS) are available.

| Income Slab            | Tax Rate |
|------------------------|----------|
| Up to ₹4,00,000        | 0%       |
| ₹4,00,001 – ₹8,00,000 | 5%       |
| ₹8,00,001 – ₹12,00,000| 10%      |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹16,00,000| 15%     |
| ₹16,00,001 – ₹20,00,000| 20%     |
| ₹20,00,001 – ₹24,00,000| 25%     |
| Above ₹24,00,000       | 30%      |

Standard deduction under new regime: ₹75,000 (Budget 2024).

## Old Tax Regime Slabs

| Income Slab                 | Tax Rate |
|-----------------------------|----------|
| Up to ₹2,50,000             | 0%       |
| ₹2,50,001 – ₹5,00,000      | 5%       |
| ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000     | 20%      |

Standard deduction under old regime: ₹50,000.

## Section 87A Rebate — AY 2026-27

### New Regime Rebate
- Applicable if total income (excluding special-rate income) does not exceed ₹12,00,000
- Maximum rebate: ₹60,000
- Effect: Zero income tax if total income ≤ ₹12,00,000 under new regime
- CRITICAL: Rebate does NOT apply to STCG under Section 111A or LTCG under Section 112A — these are taxed at flat rates regardless

### Old Regime Rebate
- Applicable if total income does not exceed ₹5,00,000
- Maximum rebate: ₹12,500
- Rebate does NOT apply to special-rate capital gains (111A, 112A)

### Marginal Relief near the 87A Threshold
When total income is just above ₹12,00,000 (new regime), marginal relief applies:
- Tax is capped at: (Total Income − ₹12,00,000)
- Example: Income ₹12,10,000 → slab tax would be ₹61,500 but is capped at ₹10,000

## Surcharge on Income Tax

| Total Income           | New Regime | Old Regime |
|------------------------|------------|------------|
| ₹50L – ₹1 Cr          | 10%        | 10%        |
| ₹1 Cr – ₹2 Cr         | 15%        | 15%        |
| ₹2 Cr – ₹5 Cr         | 25%        | 25%        |
| Above ₹5 Cr           | 25%        | 37%        |

**Surcharge on STCG (111A) and LTCG (112A): capped at 15%** regardless of income level. This is a significant benefit for high-income earners with large equity gains.

## Health and Education Cess

4% on (income tax + surcharge). Applies to all taxpayers.

## Age-Based Slab Differences (Old Regime Only)

| Category         | Condition         | Basic Exemption |
|------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Individual       | Age < 60          | ₹2,50,000       |
| Senior Citizen   | Age 60 – 79       | ₹3,00,000       |
| Super Senior     | Age ≥ 80          | ₹5,00,000       |

Age is computed as of 31-March-2026 (end of FY 2025-26).
New regime has a flat ₹4,00,000 basic exemption regardless of age.
