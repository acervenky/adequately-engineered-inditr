# Virtual Digital Assets (Crypto), ESOPs, and Special Income — AY 2026-27

## Virtual Digital Assets (VDA) — Section 115BBH

From FY 2022-23 onwards, all gains from Virtual Digital Assets (cryptocurrency, NFTs, tokens) are taxed under Section 115BBH.

### Tax Rate
- **30% flat rate** + 4% cess = effective 31.2%
- Plus surcharge if applicable (based on total income)
- Rate is the same regardless of holding period — no LTCG/STCG distinction for VDA
- No distinction between short-term and long-term holding — 30% always

### What Counts as VDA
- Bitcoin, Ethereum, and all other cryptocurrencies
- Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs)
- Any other token, digital asset as notified by the government
- Does NOT include gift cards, loyalty points (these are governed differently)

### Key Restrictions (No Set-Off, No Carry Forward)
- **No deduction** permitted except cost of acquisition
- Brokerage/trading fees cannot be deducted
- **NO set-off** of VDA losses against any other income (salary, other CG, business)
- **NO carry forward** of VDA losses to future years
- Each VDA (Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.) treated separately — losses in one cannot offset gains in another

### TDS on VDA — Section 194S
- Buyer of VDA must deduct **1% TDS** on the transaction value
- Applies if transaction value > ₹50,000/year (or ₹10,000 for some categories)
- Crypto exchanges in India deduct this at source
- TDS credit appears in Form 26AS — claim it while filing ITR
- If using foreign exchanges: no TDS deducted; you must pay full tax via advance tax / self-assessment

### How to Compute VDA Tax
1. Sum all profitable trades: total gains = gross sell price − cost of acquisition
2. Apply 30% flat rate on total gains
3. Add 4% cess
4. Add surcharge if total income > ₹50L
5. NO set-off with losses from other trades or income heads
6. Credit TDS already deducted (from 26AS)

### Reporting in ITR
- ITR-2 (Schedule VDA): must report each VDA transaction separately (exchange, date, amount)
- This disqualifies you from ITR-1 — any VDA income → must file ITR-2
- Penalties for non-disclosure are significant: 200% of tax payable for concealment

### VDA Gifts
- VDA received as gift: if value > ₹50,000 and from non-relative, taxable as "Income from Other Sources" at slab rate in the year of receipt
- Subsequent sale: cost basis = FMV on date of gift; gain taxed at 30%

### Common Pitfalls
- **Foreign exchanges (Binance, Kraken, etc.)**: no TDS but tax obligation remains; file Schedule FA (Foreign Assets) if holdings > ₹1L
- **Airdrops and staking rewards**: treated as income at FMV on receipt date, taxed at slab rate; subsequent sale taxed at 30% on gain
- **P2P trades**: must report even if no exchange involved
- **Portfolio tracker exports**: use these for cost-basis documentation; FIFO method recommended

## ESOPs (Employee Stock Option Plans) and RSUs

### Two-Stage Taxation
ESOP/RSU taxation happens at two stages:

**Stage 1 — Exercise/Vesting (Perquisite)**
- When you exercise an ESOP or when RSUs vest: the difference between Fair Market Value (FMV) on exercise/vesting date and the exercise price is treated as a **perquisite (salary income)**
- Taxed at your slab rate in the year of exercise
- Employer deducts TDS on this perquisite and shows it in Form 16 / Form 12BA
- Formula: Perquisite = (FMV on exercise date − Exercise price) × Number of shares

**Stage 2 — Sale (Capital Gains)**
- When you sell the shares, the gain from exercise value to sale price is capital gains
- Cost basis = FMV on exercise date (the price you were taxed on)
- If listed equity: STCG (< 12 months from exercise) at 20% (Section 111A) or LTCG (≥ 12 months) at 12.5% above ₹1.25L (Section 112A)
- If unlisted equity: STCG at slab rate (< 24 months), LTCG at 12.5% (≥ 24 months) — ITR-2 required for unlisted shares

### Startup ESOP — Deferred TDS (Section 192)
- Eligible startups: TDS on ESOP perquisite can be deferred for up to 48 months from exercise date OR until shares are sold, whichever is earlier
- Conditions: startup must be recognised by DPIIT; employee must be resident; shares must be from allotment after April 1, 2020
- Employee pays deferred tax in the year of the triggering event (sale or 48 months)

### RSU Taxation (Foreign Listed Companies)
- RSUs from MNCs (Google, Microsoft, Infosys ADR, etc.): FMV at vesting in USD/GBP converted to INR at RBI rate on vesting date
- Perquisite = (FMV at vesting in INR − 0) × Number of RSUs (exercise price typically ₹0)
- Sale in USD: convert sale price to INR; capital gains = Sale INR − FMV INR at vesting
- Foreign shares held: must file Schedule FA if value > ₹1L; must declare in FEMA compliance

### Key Action Items for ESOP/RSU holders
- Obtain Form 12BA from employer showing perquisite value — verify it's included in Form 16
- Keep record of grant date, vesting date, exercise date, FMV on each date
- Track holding period from exercise date (not grant date) for CG classification

## Section 80CCH — Agniveer Corpus Fund

Section 80CCH was introduced from AY 2023-24 for Agniveer scheme (Agnipath military scheme).

- Contributions made by the individual Agniveer to the Agniveer Corpus Fund are fully deductible under 80CCH
- The Central Government's matching contribution is also exempt
- Amount received from the fund on exit or discharge: **completely tax-free**
- Available under BOTH old and new regimes
- This deduction appears in Form 16 Part B line items for Agniveer defence personnel

## NPS Vatsalya (Budget 2024)

Section 80CCD(1B) was extended to include contributions to **NPS Vatsalya** — an NPS account opened for a minor child by a parent/guardian.

- Maximum deduction: ₹50,000 per year under 80CCD(1B) (same limit, shared with employee NPS contributions)
- Available under old regime only
- When the child turns 18: account converts to regular NPS Tier-1 automatically
- Good for parents wanting to start retirement savings for children early

## Winnings from Online Gaming — Section 115BBJ

From FY 2023-24, winnings from online games (Dream11, Rummy, fantasy sports, online poker) are taxed at **30% flat** (Section 115BBJ), same as VDA:
- TDS at 30% on net winnings > ₹100 per withdrawal (Section 194BA)
- No deduction except entry fee as cost
- No set-off with losses
- Reported under "Income from Other Sources" Schedule

## Foreign Assets and FBAR

If you hold any foreign assets (foreign bank accounts, RSUs in foreign companies, crypto on foreign exchanges, foreign property):
- Must declare in Schedule FA of ITR-2
- Penalty for non-disclosure: ₹10L under Black Money Act
- DTAA (Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement): if tax paid in foreign country, claim credit under Section 91/90 via Schedule TR
