# Deductions and Exemptions — AY 2026-27

## Standard Deduction

- New regime: ₹75,000 (increased from ₹50,000 via Budget 2024)
- Old regime: ₹50,000
- Available to all salaried employees and pensioners automatically
- No proof required; deducted from gross salary

## Section 80C — Investments and Payments (Old Regime Only)

Maximum deduction: ₹1,50,000 per financial year. Eligible instruments:
- ELSS mutual funds (3-year lock-in, equity exposure, best for wealth building)
- PPF (Public Provident Fund) — 15-year lock-in, ~7.1% p.a., EEE tax status
- EPF (Employee Provident Fund) — employer + employee contributions
- NSC (National Savings Certificate)
- Life insurance premium (own/spouse/children)
- ULIP premiums
- Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana
- Home loan principal repayment
- Children's tuition fees (up to 2 children)
- 5-year bank FD or Post Office FD
- Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS)

Not available under new regime.

## Section 80CCD(1B) — NPS Employee Contribution (Old Regime Only)

- Additional ₹50,000 deduction for contributions to NPS Tier-1
- This is OVER AND ABOVE the ₹1,50,000 limit of Section 80C
- Total with 80C: up to ₹2,00,000 deduction
- Not available under new regime
- NPS Tier-1 is locked until age 60; 60% lump sum tax-free at maturity, 40% annuity

## Section 80CCD(2) — Employer NPS Contribution (Both Regimes)

- Employer's NPS contribution is deductible under BOTH old and new regimes
- From FY 2025-26 (Budget 2025): limit raised to 14% of salary (Basic + DA) for all employees
  - Previously: 10% for private sector, 14% for government employees
  - Now uniformly 14% for everyone
- This arrives pre-deducted via Form 16 Part B; not counted in Chapter VI-A separately

## Section 80D — Health Insurance Premiums (Old Regime Only)

| Who is covered           | Limit (assessee < 60) | Limit (assessee ≥ 60) |
|--------------------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| Self, spouse, children   | ₹25,000               | ₹50,000               |
| Parents (< 60 years)     | ₹25,000               | —                     |
| Parents (≥ 60 years)     | ₹50,000               | ₹50,000               |

Preventive health check-up: ₹5,000 included within above limits.
Cash payments not allowed (except preventive check-up).

## HRA Exemption — Section 10(13A) (Old Regime Only)

Minimum of three conditions:
1. Actual HRA received from employer
2. Rent paid annually − 10% of (Basic Salary + DA)
3. 50% of (Basic + DA) for metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai) OR 40% for non-metro

Not available under new regime. If you live in your own house, HRA exemption is zero.
Rent receipts required for claims above ₹1,00,000/year; landlord PAN mandatory above ₹1L/year.

## Section 24(b) — Home Loan Interest (Old Regime Only)

- Self-occupied property: up to ₹2,00,000 deduction on home loan interest
- Let-out property: actual interest paid (no cap), but net HP loss capped at ₹2,00,000 offset against salary
- Under construction: interest deductible in 5 equal instalments from year of completion
- New regime: home loan interest deduction is NOT available for self-occupied property

## Section 80TTA and 80TTB — Savings Interest (Old Regime Only)

### 80TTA (Non-Senior Citizens)
- Savings bank interest up to ₹10,000 deductible
- Applies to savings a/c interest only (not FD interest)
- Not available under new regime

### 80TTB (Senior Citizens, Age ≥ 60)
- All bank interest (savings + FD + RD) up to ₹50,000 deductible
- Replaces 80TTA for senior citizens; cannot claim both
- Not available under new regime

## Section 80E — Education Loan Interest

- Interest on education loan for higher education (self/spouse/children/legal ward)
- Full interest deductible for up to 8 years from the year repayment starts
- No principal deduction; only interest
- Old regime only

## Section 80GG — Rent Paid Without HRA (Old Regime Only)

For salaried employees who do NOT receive HRA from employer but pay rent:
- Deduction is the LEAST of:
  1. ₹5,000 per month (₹60,000 per year)
  2. 25% of total income (after deductions)
  3. Rent paid annually − 10% of total income
- Conditions: must not own residential property in city of employment; spouse/minor child also must not own property there
- Not available under new regime
- Not available if HRA is part of salary package

## Family Pension Standard Deduction (Both Regimes)

Recipients of family pension (pension received by family members after death of government employee):
- Standard deduction: lower of ₹15,000 or 1/3 of family pension received
- This is different from salary standard deduction (₹75,000 / ₹50,000)
- Family pension is taxed under "Income from Other Sources" not salary
- Applicable under both old and new regimes

## Section 80G — Donations

- 100% or 50% deduction depending on the institution
- PM Relief Fund, CM Relief Fund: 100% deduction
- Some NGOs: 50% deduction with 10% of adjusted gross total income cap
- Cash donations above ₹2,000 not allowed

## Leave Travel Allowance (LTA)

- Exempt for travel within India (economy class airfare or AC-1 rail)
- 2 journeys in a block of 4 calendar years
- Block 2022–2025 ended December 2025. **Block 2026–2029 started January 2026.**
- Journeys taken in FY 2025-26 (April 2025 – March 2026): Apr-Dec 2025 belong to the 2022-2025 block; Jan-Mar 2026 belong to the new 2026-2029 block
- Any unused journeys from the 2022-2025 block can be carried over — the first journey of the 2026-2029 block must then be used in the calendar year 2026
- LTA is exempt only for actual travel costs (economy class air / AC-1 rail); no cash exemption
- Not available under new regime

## Form 12BB — Declaration to Employer (Old Regime)

Employees claiming deductions under old regime must submit Form 12BB to their employer:
- Submitted once at the start of the financial year (April), updated mid-year if needed
- Required to claim: HRA (with landlord name/PAN/rent amount), home loan interest, 80C, LIC, LTA
- Without Form 12BB, employer deducts TDS on full salary without applying deductions
- Deductions can still be claimed at filing time even without Form 12BB, but you must pay any TDS shortfall

## Section 89(1) — Relief for Arrears and Advance Salary

If you received salary arrears, leave encashment, or gratuity in FY 2025-26 for services rendered in earlier years:
- Total income for the year is inflated, pushing you into a higher tax bracket
- Section 89(1) provides relief to ensure you're not worse off than if income was received in the year earned
- **Must file Form 10E on IT portal BEFORE filing ITR** — failure to do so results in relief being disallowed
- Applicable to: salary arrears, advance salary, leave encashment on resignation, gratuity excess

## Tax-Free Allowances Under New Regime

Standard deduction (₹75,000) and employer NPS 80CCD(2) (14% of salary) are the only deductions under new regime.
All other allowances (HRA, LTA, food coupons, phone allowance, etc.) are fully taxable under new regime.
Under old regime: HRA, LTA, meal coupons (₹2,200/month), phone reimbursement (actual), books (actual) are partially or fully exempt.
