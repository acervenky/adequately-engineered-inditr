# Capital Gains Rules — AY 2026-27 (FY 2025-26)

## Overview of Capital Gains Types

Capital gains arise when a capital asset (shares, MFs, property, gold, etc.) is sold for a profit.
Taxed separately from regular income at special flat rates.
Budget 2024 significantly changed CG rates effective 23 July 2024.
Budget 2026 kept these rates unchanged for AY 2026-27.

## Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG)

### Section 111A — Listed Equity Shares and Equity MFs
- Asset: equity shares (listed), equity-oriented mutual funds, units of business trust
- Holding period: less than 12 months
- Tax rate: **20%** (raised from 15% by Budget 2024, effective 23 July 2024)
- 87A rebate does NOT apply to 111A income
- Surcharge capped at 15%

### Other STCG (Non-Equity)
- Debt MF, property, gold, unlisted shares held < 36 months
- Taxed at the **slab rate** of the taxpayer
- 87A rebate applies

## Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG)

### Section 112A — Listed Equity Shares and Equity MFs
- Asset: equity shares (listed), equity-oriented mutual funds, STT paid
- Holding period: 12 months or more
- Tax rate: **12.5%** on gains ABOVE ₹1,25,000 annual exemption (Budget 2024)
- Exemption of ₹1,25,000 (raised from ₹1,00,000) resets each financial year
- 87A rebate does NOT apply to 112A income
- Surcharge capped at 15%
- No indexation benefit
- LTCG annual harvesting strategy: book up to ₹1,25,000 in LTCG every year tax-free; sell and rebuy to reset cost basis

### Section 112 — Property and Other Assets (Post Jul 23, 2024)

#### Property acquired or sold AFTER 23 July 2024
- Tax rate: **12.5% without indexation**
- No choice — indexation benefit removed by Budget 2024

#### Property acquired BEFORE 23 July 2024
- Choice available: lower of:
  - 12.5% without indexation (new rate)
  - 20% with indexation using Cost Inflation Index (old rate)
- Engine computes both and applies whichever gives lower tax
- Indexation uses Cost Inflation Index (CII):
  - FY 2024-25: **363**
  - FY 2025-26: **376** (notified by CBDT on 1 July 2025 — use this for AY 2026-27 property indexation)
- Indexed cost = (Original cost × CII of year of sale) ÷ CII of year of acquisition
- Example: Property bought in FY 2010-11 (CII=167) for ₹40L, sold in FY 2025-26 (CII=376):
  Indexed cost = 40L × 376 ÷ 167 = ₹90.06L → indexed gain = sale price − ₹90.06L → tax at 20%

### Debt Mutual Funds (Section 50AA — Finance Act 2023)
- MFs where equity holding is less than 65% — taxed at **slab rate**
- Applies regardless of holding period — even 10+ year debt MF is taxed at slab rate
- This rule applies to units purchased on or after 1 April 2023
- Units purchased BEFORE 1 April 2023: still taxed at LTCG rates if held > 36 months (12.5% without indexation for acquisitions before Jul 23, 2024)

## Holding Period Rules

| Asset Type                         | STCG Period  | LTCG Period  |
|------------------------------------|--------------|--------------|
| Listed equity shares               | < 12 months  | ≥ 12 months  |
| Equity-oriented MFs (STT paid)     | < 12 months  | ≥ 12 months  |
| Debt MFs (post Apr 2023)           | Any period → slab rate (Section 50AA) |
| Unlisted equity shares             | < 24 months  | ≥ 24 months  |
| Immovable property                 | < 24 months  | ≥ 24 months  |
| Gold, jewellery                    | < 24 months  | ≥ 24 months  |
| Bonds (listed)                     | < 12 months  | ≥ 12 months  |

## ITR-2 Split Reporting (AY 2026-27 Clarification)

For AY 2025-26, taxpayers had to separately report pre/post 23 July 2024 transactions in ITR-2 (transitional requirement).
For AY 2026-27, this pre/post split reporting is no longer required — all FY 2025-26 transactions use the uniform Budget 2024 rates.

## Set-off and Carry Forward Rules

- STCG can be set off against STCG or LTCG losses
- LTCG can only be set off against LTCG losses
- Unabsorbed CG losses can be carried forward for 8 assessment years
- Brought-forward CG losses disqualify ITR-1 filers — must file ITR-2

## Securities Transaction Tax (STT)

- Listed equity transactions attract STT at source
- STT paid is a pre-condition for 111A and 112A concessional rates
- STT is NOT deductible as a business expense for investors

## Share Buyback — Budget 2025-26 Change

From FY 2025-26, proceeds from share buyback are taxed as capital gains (not dividend):
- Same holding period and rate rules as equity LTCG/STCG apply
- Previously taxed as dividend with DDT borne by company; now shareholder is taxed

## CG Reinvestment Exemptions (Old and New Regime Both)

These exemptions reduce LTCG tax by reinvesting sale proceeds. Available under BOTH regimes.

### Section 54 — Residential Property into Residential Property
- Applies when: LTCG from sale of a residential house property
- Exemption: invest in ONE new residential property in India
- Timeline: buy within 1 year before or 2 years after sale; or construct within 3 years after sale
- Limit: no cap on exemption amount (full LTCG exempt if full proceeds reinvested)
- If new property sold within 3 years: exemption reversed and taxed as STCG
- Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS): unutilised proceeds must be deposited before filing deadline

### Section 54EC — Property LTCG into Specified Bonds
- Applies when: LTCG from sale of any immovable property (land or building)
- Exemption: invest in NHAI or REC bonds (Section 54EC bonds)
- Limit: maximum ₹50,00,000 per financial year
- Timeline: within 6 months of sale
- Lock-in: 5 years — if redeemed early, exemption reversed
- Interest on bonds: taxable at slab rate; no TDS if interest ≤ ₹5,000/year per bond

### Section 54F — Non-Residential Asset LTCG into Residential Property
- Applies when: LTCG from sale of any capital asset OTHER than residential property (e.g., gold, unlisted shares, commercial property)
- Exemption: proportional to amount invested in ONE new residential property
- Condition: must not own more than one residential house on date of sale (excluding new property)
- Timeline: buy within 1 year before or 2 years after; or construct within 3 years
- Full exemption if net sale consideration fully reinvested; partial if partially reinvested

### Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS)
- If reinvestment not done before filing ITR: deposit unutilised amount in CGAS bank account
- Withdrawal only for the specified purpose (buying/constructing property)
- Must be utilised within the prescribed timelines; else treated as capital gain of that year

## Advance Tax on Capital Gains

Capital gains realised during the year should be included in advance tax estimates:
- Q1 (Jun 15): 15% of estimated annual tax
- Q2 (Sep 15): 45% cumulative
- Q3 (Dec 15): 75% cumulative
- Q4 (Mar 15): 100% cumulative
- Interest under 234C is charged if advance tax is short by more than 10%
